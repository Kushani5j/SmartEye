Research Gap

It could be seen that many researches were carried out to aid the blind to engage in their day to day activities with confidence and free from risks. Those researches carried out beforehand had been able to succeed to a certain extent, but limitations and restrictions could be cited in them which laid the foundation for our research. After the completion of the background and literature survey, many gaps among the proposed solution and the solutions implemented beforehand could be recognized.
Most of the solutions those were implemented earlier had a similar face compared to the proposed solution, out of which the scope of those solutions was cited as the main limitation or the restriction that widened the gap between the proposed system and those solutions. Furthermore, features developed, or the functionalities integrated into those implementations were minimal when compared to the proposed solution. Limitations and restriction in the technologies at those times of development should not be disregarded, as the hardware infrastructures and software advancements present as per today, did not exist back then.
As mentioned beforehand, a fact that could be perceived from literature review was that, researches conducted formerly did not comprise a widened scope. Most of the researches those were conducted had a minimal targeted goal or objective. Even though, those implementations and researches were very valuable, effective, efficient, and extremely helpful, when compared to the proposed system that is to be implemented, it has to be cited that the proposed system has a much more widened scope comparative to previous systems. The reason to why the scope of the system matters is that, then it does not require to halt after a certain extent, by which can assist reach objectives or goals of a higher level.
Different products were developed as results of numerous researches, which is capable of catering solutions for a variety of problems faced by visionless individuals, of which each of them considers only a certain aspect of the whole picture. This is where their drawbacks can be cited, as a totally visionless individual need many assistive techniques to support them to carry out their day to day activities with the least or the minimal amount of external aid, which would allow them to live independently in the society. A fact that could be uncovered by following the literature survey was that, there was no all in one solution developed which is capable of providing assistance for a visionless to satisfy all their requirements. This is yet another underpinning circumstance that is capable of giving the proposed system the upper hand when considering the research gap.
When reviewing the research gap, the technological advancements that has taken place over the last few years cannot be disregarded, as it has been an immense evolution in terms of hardware infrastructure and software. Absence of such infrastructure during earlier research eras could also be taken into account when evaluating the research gap. Absence or the unawareness of Internet of Things concepts, microcontrollers, advanced image processing libraries, and machine learning algorithms could be cited as some examples of research gap widening factors, which are trending topics as at present, and are also used in the proposed system. These factors will directly or indirectly affect the final output, in which can cause the whole system to be less effective and efficient.
